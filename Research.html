Expoxyketone Paper Research Support

This project centered around searching scientific databases such as NP Atlas and research articles for information on natural and synthetic compounds containing a specific substructure. To support paper writing efforts on the pharmacological importance of these compounds, any information regarding the molecule and its pharmacological activity were recorded in a comprehensive spreadsheet.


However, after about 350 compounds had been used to populate the spreadsheet, it became apparent that the criteria for what is considered biologically active had to be revised to fit more appropriate and promising standards if these compounds were to eventually be considered for pharmacological use. These revision steps are ongoing in conjunction with efforts to add more compounds to the spreadsheet.


For further contact and inquiries about this project, please reach out through the Contact page or send an email to tberger@ucsd.edu.



Potential Therapeutics for Traumatic Brain Injury

This project was part of the work supporting efforts to produe an FDA-approved pharmaceutical to combat secondary injuries following traumatic brain injury. Inhibition of two human enzymes showed promise as targets for this treatment: cathepsin B and L. Another related enzyme found in the malaria parasite was also studied: cruzain.


Given that this project focused on effective enzyme inhibition, the information used to populate this spreadsheet focused on the kinetic parameters of the method by which these compounds inhibit its target enzyme.


Currently, this project is being expanded to include the pH environment at which these compounds inhibit their target enzymes as this parameter is integral to apply these compounds inhibitive properties to new pharmaceuticals.
